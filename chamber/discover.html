In 1870s Freight road was established as a main route from the railraod in Kelton, Utah, headed towards Boise, Idaho and the mining towns in central Idaho. This route broute travelers through what is now Albion.

Settlers began filing for homesteads in the Albion area in 1871.

The Methodist congregation was established in 1878 and the Albion Methodist Church was built in 1888-89. It became Albion's first institutional building; it remained one of the city's few public buildings for much of its early history and hosted other churches' services, public meetings. The church remained open through 1951,

The building is now listed on the National Register of Historic Places and serves as the community grange hall.

In the 1890s Idaho was in need of teachers. The State established two Normal schools. One in Northern Idaho, Lewiston, and the other in Southern Idaho in Albion.

In 1893 Albion State Normal School was established as a teachers college. It remained open until 1947.

Today Albion has a population of 422 people. Agriculture, mostly ranches, fill the valley. Albion is the gateway to the Albion Mountains. Mt Harrison is the highest peak at 9,265 feet. Pomerelle Ski Resort is only 16 miles from Albion.

Visit the Normal School Museum to discover all you can about the fascinating history of Albion State Normal School.

Want to see the old state school campus? Visit the Albion Campus Retreat or the Haunted Mansions of Albion to see the ongoing restoration of the campus.
