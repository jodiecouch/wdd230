In 1870s a freight route was established from the railraod in Kelton, Utah, headed towards Boise, Idaho and the mining towns in that area. This route broute travelers through what is now Albion. Settlers began filing for homesteads in the Albion area in 1871.

The City of Rocks Backcountry Byway weaves through the Albion Mountain Range and rural landscapes peppered with towering granite spires.

The Backcountry Byway Begins in Albion on Idaho highway 77, the byway passes by Pomerelle Mountain Resort and Lake Cleveland Recreation Area as it heads south. Attracting rock climbers, campers and hikers, the City of Rocks National Reserve and the neighboring Castle Rocks State Park feature enchanting geologic wonders.

Today Albion has a population of 422 people. Agriculture, mostly ranches, fill the valley. However, Albion remains the gateway to the Albion Mountains. Mt Harrison is the highest peak at 9,265 feet. Pomerelle Ski Resort is only 16 miles from Albion. The City of Rocks is 29 miles from Albion.

The Methodist congregation was established in 1878 and the Albion Methodist Church was built in 1888-89. It became Albion's first institutional building; it remained one of the city's few public buildings for much of its early history and hosted other churches' services and public meetings. The church remained open through 1951,

The building was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1986. Today the build is Grange Hall #321 and still used a public meeting place for the community.

In the 1890s Idaho was in need of teachers. The State established two Normal schools. One in Northern Idaho, in Lewiston, and the other in Southern Idaho, in Albion.

In 1893 Albion State Normal School was established as a teachers college. It remained open until 1947. You can visit the Normal School Museum to discover all you can about the fascinating history of Albion State Normal School.

Want to see the old state school campus? Visit the Albion Campus Retreat or the Haunted Mansions of Albion to see the ongoing restoration of the campus.

Albion is a community rich in history. The restored campus along with the many historic buildings still in use throughout Albion bring the rich history of the area alive to travelers of today.
