White Space and Fitt's Law

Capital One

https://www.capitalone.com/

Capital One has been at the fore-front of great user design. Simple is how I would describe Capital One's website and their mobile app. They use white space to make things easy to find and easy to read. In this example they also use Fitt's Law effectively, the continue button almost jumps out at the user. It takes effort not to click it.

Contrast

Verizon Wireless

https://www.verizon.com/

Verizon Wireless uses white space, or negative space well. Really key here though is their use of contrast to highlight how they want you to feel about their products and services. If you follow the link you will see that they captivate the user with contrast and motion.

Hick's Law

StackOverflow

https://stackoverflow.com/

StackOverflow is more like software rather than a website that is pushing a product or service. StackOverflow isn't pushing you to buy anything and their content has a nice usable business type software feel to it. Usable is how I would describe their landing page. I think Hick's law applies here because when I look at the page there is one button. It is the most colorful and it is right at the top. It feels as if there is only one choice, to ask a question.
